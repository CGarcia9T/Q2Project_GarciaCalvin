Bibingka

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour (or glutinous rice flour for a chewier texture)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk
  • ½ cup fresh milk (or evaporated milk)
  • ½ cup butter (melted)
  • 1 cup grated cheese (optional, for topping)
  • 2 salted eggs (sliced, optional, for topping)
  • Banana leaves (wilted over an open flame for pliability, optional)
  • Butter or margarine (for greasing)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the baking pan: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking pan or individual molds with banana leaves, greasing the leaves lightly with butter. If banana leaves are unavailable, grease the pan directly.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the rice flour, all-purpose flour, sugar, and baking powder. Mix well.
  3. Combine the wet ingredients: In another bowl, whisk the eggs, coconut milk, fresh milk, and melted butter until smooth.
  4. Make the batter: Gradually add the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until a smooth, lump-free batter forms.
  5. Pour into the pan: Pour the batter into the prepared pan or molds, filling about ¾ full to allow space for rising.
  6. Add toppings: Arrange slices of salted eggs and sprinkle grated cheese on top of the batter.
  7. Bake: Place the pan in the oven and bake for 20–25 minutes or until the bibingka is set and lightly golden on top. To check doneness, insert a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean.
  8. Brush with butter: Once done, remove from the oven and brush the top with butter while still warm.
  9. Serve and enjoy: Serve the bibingka warm or at room temperature, optionally with grated coconut on the side.
