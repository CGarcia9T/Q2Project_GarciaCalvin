Ube Halaya

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups Ube (purple yam), boiled and mashed
  • 1 can (14 oz) Sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 oz) Evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup Butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup Granulated sugar (optional, adjust to sweetness)
  • 1/4 teaspoon Vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the ube: Boil the ube in water until soft, then peel and mash it well.
  2. Combine ingredients: In a saucepan, mix the mashed ube, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, melted butter, sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Cook the mixture: Over low to medium heat, cook the ube mixture, stirring constantly to prevent it from sticking or burning. Cook for about 15-20 minutes until it thickens and becomes smooth.
  4. Cool down: Once the mixture reaches a smooth, thick consistency, remove it from the heat. Let it cool for a few minutes.
  5. Chill: Transfer the ube halaya into a mold or container and let it cool completely. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours to set.
  6. Serve: Once chilled, serve as a dessert or use it as a topping for other Filipino treats like halo-halo.
