Menudo

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 lb Beef liver, cut into small cubes (optional)
  • 2 medium Potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 medium Carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup Red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup Green peas (optional)
  • 1 large Onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons Soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup Tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup Tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup Vinegar
  • 1/2 cup Water or broth
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon Fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Cooking oil for sautéing

Instructions

  1. Marinate the pork: In a bowl, combine the pork with soy sauce, vinegar, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Let it marinate for about 15-20 minutes.
  2. Sauté aromatics: In a large pot, heat oil and sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant.
  3. Brown the pork: Add the marinated pork to the pot and cook until it is lightly browned.
  4. Add vegetables: Add the potatoes, carrots, red bell pepper, and bay leaves. Stir to combine.
  5. Add liquids: Pour in the tomato sauce, tomato paste, and water (or broth). Stir well and let it simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the pork is tender.
  6. Add liver and peas: If using, add the beef liver and green peas and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes.
  7. Season: Adjust with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice.
