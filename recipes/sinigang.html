Sinigang

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs pork belly (or spare ribs, cut into chunks)
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 packet tamarind soup mix (or fresh tamarind if available)
  • 1 cup radish (sliced thinly)
  • 1 cup eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach, or substitute with spinach)
  • 1 cup sitaw (yard-long beans, cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 2 medium green chili peppers (optional, for a hint of spice)
  • Salt or fish sauce (patis) to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil the pork: In a large pot, bring the water to a boil. Add the pork, onion, and tomatoes. Simmer over medium heat for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork is tender. Skim off any scum or impurities that rise to the surface.
  2. Add the tamarind mix: Once the pork is tender, add the tamarind soup mix (or fresh tamarind juice if using). Stir well and let it dissolve completely. Adjust the sourness by adding more tamarind mix if needed.
  3. Cook the vegetables: Add the radish, eggplant, and sitaw to the pot. Let them cook for 5–7 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Season the soup: Add fish sauce or salt to taste. You can also add the green chili peppers at this point for a subtle kick.
  5. Add the leafy greens: Add the kangkong (water spinach) last and let it cook for about 1–2 minutes, or until wilted.
  6. Serve and enjoy: Ladle the sinigang into bowls and serve hot with steamed rice.
